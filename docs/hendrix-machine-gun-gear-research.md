# Jimi Hendrix "Machine Gun" - Band of Gypsys Gear Research

## Performance Context
- **Recording**: Live at Fillmore East, New York City
- **Date**: January 1, 1970 (2nd show, New Year's Day) - the iconic 12-minute version
- **Band**: Band of Gypsys (Hendrix, Billy Cox bass, Buddy Miles drums)
- **Also recorded**: December 31, 1969 (two shows) and January 1, 1970 (two shows) - four total sets
- **Album**: "Band of Gypsys" released March 25, 1970
- **Sound engineer**: Eddie Kramer (remixed for later releases)
- **Marshall rep Marc Franklin** had backstage access, documenting the concerts

---

## 1. GUITAR

### Fender Stratocaster "Black Beauty" [CONFIRMED]
- **Model**: 1968 Fender Stratocaster
- **Finish**: Black with white pickguard
- **Neck**: Maple cap fretboard
- **Electronics**: STOCK - no modifications to pickups or electronics
- **Pickups**: Original 1968 Fender single-coil pickups
- **Setup**: Right-handed guitar played left-handed (flipped/strung upside-down)
  - This reverses the bridge pickup slant: treble strings get less brightness, bass strings get more
  - Nut and bridge would have been re-cut for reversed string order
- **Strings**: Fender Rock 'N' Roll light gauge: .010, .013, .015, .026, .032, .038
- **Tuning**: Approximately D standard (full step down) or between D and Eb
  - Billy Cox confirmed they tuned down intentionally "to save Jimi's voice"
  - The BOG recordings sit between D and Eb, slightly closer to D
  - This is LOWER than Hendrix's typical Eb tuning from the Experience era
- **Pickup position for Machine Gun**: Primarily neck pickup (position 5 on reversed guitar) and neck+middle combination (position 4)
  - Neck pickup for warm, thick lead tones
  - Tone knob rolled back for some sections (confirmed by multiple analyses)
- **Whammy bar**: Heavily used - the famous sustained trill sections use bar manipulation
- **Confidence**: HIGH - photographic evidence and forum consensus confirm Black Beauty at BOG

## 2. SIGNAL CHAIN ORDER [CONFIRMED]

**Guitar --> Vox Wah (Mayer-modded) --> Roger Mayer Octavia --> Fuzz (Axis Fuzz in Fuzz Face enclosure) --> Uni-Vibe --> Marshall amps**

This is the "BOG pedals" or "Voodoo pedals" configuration. Key points:
- **Photographic evidence** exists showing the exact cable routing at the Fillmore
- The wah's output cable is visibly plugged into the Octavia
- The Uni-Vibe is LAST in the chain before the amp (confirmed by multiple sources)
- This is DIFFERENT from the earlier Experience-era chain (Wah --> Fuzz Face --> Octavia)
- The Octavia was moved BETWEEN the wah and fuzz (previously it was after the fuzz)
- Roger Mayer returned as Hendrix's live technician specifically for these shows

### Why Uni-Vibe Last?
- All gain/fuzz/octave effects PRECEDE the Uni-Vibe
- This means the Uni-Vibe processes an already-distorted, harmonically rich signal
- The phase-shifting creates a more complex, swirling modulation on the overtone-laden signal
- Multiple sources note this placement "amplifies its sound" - the fuzz/octave drives the Uni-Vibe harder

### Signal Chain Confidence: HIGH
- Multiple independent sources (Custom Boards, Effectrode, GuitarPlayer, forum analyses) confirm this order
- Photographic evidence from the Fillmore shows supports it
- Roger Mayer's own accounts are consistent with this

## 3. EFFECTS PEDALS - DETAILED BREAKDOWN

### 3a. Wah-Wah Pedal [CONFIRMED]
- **Model**: Vox Cry Baby, modified by Roger Mayer
- **Modifications**: Roger Mayer modified with "proprietary RM dual wound inductor" and component upgrades
  - Mayer's wah modifications date back to the beginning of his relationship with Hendrix
  - The 9090A upgrade circuit is the commercial version of these mods
- **Physical mod**: Missing bottom-left rubber foot (removed intentionally for comfortable tilt angle)
- **Frequency sweep**: Approximately 450 Hz (heel down) to 1600 Hz (toe down), center ~750 Hz
- **Usage on Machine Gun**:
  - Expressive wah throughout - NOT parked/half-cocked
  - Dynamic sweeping synchronized with phrasing
  - Some sections with wah off entirely (clean Uni-Vibe sections)
- **Confidence**: HIGH for model, MEDIUM for exact modifications

### 3b. Roger Mayer Octavia [CONFIRMED]
- **Builder**: Roger Mayer (hand-built)
- **History**: Hendrix had lost his previous Octavia units. He called Mayer asking for one for the BOG shows. Mayer delivered a "finished, fit-for-gigging version" from a batch made earlier that year (1969)
- **Circuit**: By 1970, Mayer had redesigned the Octavia to use all-silicon transistors with NO transformer (unlike the original 1967 version which used a transformer for octave-up)
- **Function**: Frequency doubling (octave-up) combined with fuzz
- **Position in chain**: Between wah and fuzz (NOT after fuzz as in earlier configurations)
- **Usage on Machine Gun**: Used for specific sections - the upper-octave screaming passages
- **The actual BOG Octavia pedal sold at auction for $70,400** - confirming its provenance
- **Confidence**: HIGH - first-hand Roger Mayer accounts, auction documentation

### 3c. Fuzz Pedal: Roger Mayer Axis Fuzz [CONFIRMED - NOT a standard Fuzz Face]
- **External housing**: Standard Fuzz Face enclosure (round, red with white knobs)
- **Internal circuit**: Roger Mayer Axis Fuzz - completely different from a Fuzz Face
- **CRITICAL DISTINCTION**: Despite being housed in a Fuzz Face shell, the circuit inside is NOT a Fuzz Face circuit at all

#### Circuit Details of the Axis Fuzz:
- **Topology**: "Discrete circuit configuration completely unlike the simple and crude Fuzz Face"
- **Transistors**: Both PNP AND NPN silicon low-noise transistors in a "unique configuration"
- **NOT germanium** - silicon transistors by this era (temperature stable, consistent)
- **High input impedance** - preserves guitar's character (unlike standard Fuzz Face which loads the guitar)
- **More output level and sustain** than the Classic Fuzz
- **Works on chords AND solos** (standard Fuzz Face tends to be mushy on chords)
- **Temperature stable** and free from radio interference (advantages of silicon over germanium)

#### Relationship to Octavio Circuit:
- Dunlop's engineers concluded the BOG fuzz was "a version of the Octavio circuit that didn't have the octave up signal"
- The Axis Fuzz "shares DNA with the driver circuit of the Octavias"
- The JHF-3 (Dunlop recreation) is "very similar to the Axis Fuzz" with "gain kicked up a notch by changing some resistor values and adding an emitter-follower 2nd stage"
- The circuit is silicon-based, NOT the germanium PNP Fuzz Face topology

#### What This Means for Emulation:
- Do NOT model this as a standard 2-transistor PNP Ge Fuzz Face
- The circuit is closer to the Octavia driver section (minus the frequency doubler)
- Silicon transistors: sharper attack, more consistent, brighter/harder clipping than Ge
- Higher gain than standard Fuzz Face
- Better chord clarity than standard Fuzz Face
- High input impedance means it doesn't interact with guitar volume pot the same way a Fuzz Face does

- **Confidence**: HIGH for circuit type, MEDIUM for exact component values (the original is lost)

### 3d. Univox Uni-Vibe [CONFIRMED]
- **Model**: Univox Uni-Vibe (manufactured by JEN in Italy for Shin-ei)
- **Modification**: Roger Mayer "fine-tuned" it - "all I had to do was a little fine-tuning. With the correct set-up these things really sound very musical"
- **Position**: LAST in the effects chain, right before the amplifiers
- **Mode for Machine Gun**: DEBATED between Chorus and Vibrato
  - Vibrato mode = fully wet (100% phase-shifted signal) -- more extreme throbbing
  - Chorus mode = blend of dry + wet (50/50 mix) -- retains note definition
  - Some analyses say Vibrato (the deeper pulsing suggests fully wet)
  - Previous research session concluded Chorus based on retained note clarity
  - MOST LIKELY: Hendrix switched between modes during the 12-minute performance
  - For a preset, Chorus mode at ~3-5 Hz is a safe starting point (more usable, less extreme)
- **Speed**: Variable - Hendrix manipulated the speed control expressively throughout the performance
  - Matched to the tempo of the machine gun riff in the intro
  - Varied dynamically during solos
  - The foot-controlled speed pedal may have been used for real-time speed changes
- **Key sonic contribution**: The intro riff's "machine gun" firing sound is created by the Uni-Vibe's pulsing modulation on a fuzzed-out signal
- **Confidence**: HIGH for model and placement, MEDIUM for exact mode/speed settings

## 4. AMPLIFICATION

### Marshall Super Lead 100-watt (Model 1959) [CONFIRMED]
- **Model**: Marshall 1959 Super Lead, 100 watts
- **Era**: Late 1960s production (1968-1969 heads)
- **Configuration**: Three heads with six 4x12 cabinets (three full stacks)
  - Heads were daisy-chained together via the channel-linking method
  - Both channels jumped (Normal + Bright linked via patch cable)
  - Running in PARALLEL (not series)

### West Coast Organ & Amp Modifications (Dave Weyer) [CONFIRMED]
Dave Weyer, an engineer at West Coast Organ and Amplifier Service in Hollywood, CA, modified Hendrix's Marshall amps starting in 1969. In 1969, the Experience received eight new Super Lead heads and about ten 4x12 cabinets before the start of Jimi's '69 tour, all serviced through West Coast Organ. These modifications were used at Woodstock, Berkeley, the Fillmore East, and many other venues.

#### Power Tube Swap:
- **Tubes**: GE 6550A replacing stock Mullard EL34 (also Tung-Sol and RCA 6550s used)
- **Bias**: ~25mA per tube (COLD -- near Class B operation). Stock EL34 = 35-40mA
- **Plate voltage**: ~600V (stock ~480V). Higher than stock = more headroom + harder clip transition
- **Screen voltage**: ~300V
- **Screen resistors**: 470 ohm (JTM45 spec, lower than later 1K values)
- Weyer rebiased all the amps and changed suppressor-grid voltages to prevent 6550 over-dissipation
- Hendrix specifically told Weyer he "preferred the sound of 6550 tubes"

#### 6550 Cold Bias Sonic Impact (THIS IS THE #1 REASON BOG SOUNDS DIFFERENT):
- At 25mA idle with 600V plates = ~15W dissipation (well below 42W max rating)
- Near Class B = crossover distortion at zero crossing
- Creates the "gnarly, barking" quality described by Weyer
- Percussive attack transients -- tubes are essentially off between signal peaks
- Different harmonic spectrum from EL34: more even harmonics when driven, thicker midrange
- This crossover distortion is what gives the BOG tone its "tight" and "percussive" character

#### Tone Stack Modifications:
- **Slope resistor**: 33K (stock: 56K) -- more bass/mid gain through the tone stack
- **Treble cap**: 500pF (stock: 250pF) -- moderate treble boost
- **Net effect**: Shifts mid center frequency from ~800Hz down to ~600-700Hz, more bass/mid gain
- **Treble compensation**: Extra HF boost to compensate for coiled cable capacitance rolloff

#### Other Modifications:
- **Output transformer**: ~5K ohms primary (may be rewound/replaced for 6550 impedance matching)
- **Power transformer**: Oversized, well-regulated
- **Preamp filtering**: 32+32uF capacitors
- **V3 (phase inverter)**: May have been swapped to 12AT7 (lower gain, more headroom in PI)

### Amp Settings [LIKELY - not photographically confirmed for BOG specifically]
Best available estimates from multiple sources, roadie accounts, and photographic evidence:
- **Presence**: 6 (some sources say up to 9)
- **Bass**: 3-5
- **Middle**: 6-8 (high mids are KEY to the BOG tone)
- **Treble**: 4-6
- **High Treble (Channel II) Volume**: 7-8 (or higher)
- **Normal (Channel I) Volume**: 0 or low (not primary channel)
- **Channel linking**: YES - both channels jumped

### Important Amp Behavior Notes:
- Hendrix did NOT necessarily run everything dimed (at 10)
- Roadie accounts say he often started at moderate settings and turned up during the show
- By show's end, "all the dials were pegged/dimed/moved to 10"
- His tone control came significantly from guitar volume pot manipulation
  - Volume at 10: full distortion/fuzz
  - Volume rolled back 5-6: crunch for verse sections
  - Volume rolled back further: clean tones
- The 6550 + cold bias means the amps clip DIFFERENTLY from a stock Marshall:
  - More headroom before breakup (600V plates)
  - But when they DO clip, it's a harder, more abrupt transition
  - Class B crossover distortion adds "grit" even at lower volumes
- Mid-heavy settings are KEY to the BOG tone (distinguishes it from more scooped Woodstock/Monterey)

### Cabinets [CONFIRMED with caveats]:
- Marshall 1960 4x12 cabinets (mix of 1960A slant + 1960B straight)
- Six cabinets total (two per head, three full stacks)
- **Speakers**: LIKELY heavier-duty replacements, NOT stock Celestion Greenbacks
  - Hendrix blew Celestion G12M Greenbacks (25W) constantly at full volume
  - West Coast Organ replaced stock Celestions with 75-watt Rola speakers (bought from Thomas Organ)
  - POSSIBLY JBL D120F (120W handling, aluminum dust cap, flat/extended response, no speaker breakup)
  - Multiple forum sources report Hendrix moved from G12M Greenback -> G12H-30 -> JBL D120F over time
  - JBL D120F explanation: if used, this is why BOG sounds more "modern" and "hi-fi" than typical Marshall tones -- flat response, no speaker compression/distortion
  - The 75-watt Rolas from Thomas Organ may be the "missing link" between Celestions and JBLs
- **For our app**: The Greenback IR is the closest standard option; a flat/extended-response IR would be more accurate if JBLs were indeed used

### Amp Confidence: HIGH for model (1959 Super Lead), HIGH for 6550/Weyer mods (confirmed by Ceriatone recreation, Metropoulos forum), MEDIUM for exact settings, MEDIUM for speaker type

## 5. CABLES AND MISCELLANEOUS

- **Instrument cables**: Long curly/coiled cables (high capacitance)
  - These roll off treble significantly (the reason for Marshall treble mods)
  - Estimated 20-30 feet of coiled cable = substantial capacitive loading on pickups
  - This would roll off frequencies above approximately 3-5 kHz depending on cable capacitance
- **Power**: Standard US 120V/60Hz (Fillmore East, NYC)
- **No effects loop**: All effects in front of the amp (no send/return)
- **Feedback**: Hendrix extensively used controlled feedback from the amp speakers
  - Standing at specific distances/angles from the cabinets
  - This is a CRITICAL part of the Machine Gun tone - especially the sustained screaming notes
  - The long sustaining note at ~3:59 is picked only twice in 10 seconds - the rest is feedback

## 6. WHAT MAKES THE BOG TONE DIFFERENT FROM EARLIER HENDRIX

### Key Differences from Experience Era (1966-1968):
1. **6550 tubes cold-biased** (vs stock EL34 Class AB): Near Class B operation = percussive attack, crossover distortion, "gnarly barking" quality. THIS IS THE SINGLE BIGGEST FACTOR in the BOG sound difference
2. **Higher plate voltage** (600V vs 480V): More headroom + harder clip transition when the amp does break up
3. **Fuzz circuit**: Axis Fuzz (silicon, Octavio-derived) vs. standard germanium Fuzz Face
4. **Signal chain order**: Octavia moved between wah and fuzz (was after fuzz before)
5. **Uni-Vibe**: Not available/used before late 1968 - now a central part of the sound
6. **Tuning**: Down to approximately D (vs. Eb in Experience era)
7. **Midrange emphasis**: BOG tone has more prominent mids (33K slope resistor + 500pF treble cap in tone stack)
8. **Speakers**: Heavier-duty speakers (75W Rolas or JBL D120F) vs. Celestion G12M Greenbacks = less speaker breakup/compression, flatter response, more "modern" sound
9. **Silicon vs. Germanium**: The shift to silicon fuzz produces sharper attack and more consistent response
10. **Roger Mayer's return**: After being absent from Hendrix's live rig for a period, Mayer returned with refined versions of his effects
11. **Billy Cox** (finger-style Jazz Bass) vs. Noel Redding (pick Rickenbacker) = fatter low end, freed Hendrix to push midrange harder

### The "Machine Gun" Tone Specifically:
- Full, round, glassy, and shimmering (described even at low volume in the intro)
- The Uni-Vibe creates the "firing machine gun" simulation in the intro riff
- Dynamic range from whisper-quiet clean to screaming feedback
- Extensive whammy bar use creates fluid pitch modulation over trills
- Controlled feedback produces impossibly long sustain on key notes
- The combination of Octavia + fuzz creates harmonically complex upper-register screams

## 7. EFFECTS USAGE MAP WITHIN "MACHINE GUN" (12-minute version, Jan 1, 1970)

### Intro (0:00 - ~1:00): "The Machine Gun Riff"
- **Active**: Uni-Vibe (vibrato mode, speed matched to riff tempo), Fuzz (Axis Fuzz), likely wah off
- **Pickup**: Neck, tone rolled back partially
- **Character**: Pulsing, rhythmic, mimicking gunfire

### Verse/Rhythm Sections:
- **Active**: Uni-Vibe (slower speed), lighter fuzz or fuzz off
- **Guitar volume**: Rolled back for cleaner passages
- **Character**: Swirling, atmospheric, building tension

### Solo Sections (multiple throughout):
- **Active**: All effects potentially engaged - Wah (active sweeping), Fuzz (full), Octavia (for upper-octave screams), Uni-Vibe
- **Guitar volume**: Full up (10)
- **Pickup**: Neck or neck+middle
- **Character**: Aggressive, screaming, soaring

### The Sustained Note (~3:59):
- **Active**: Fuzz, amp feedback (controlled)
- **Technique**: Note picked only twice in ~10 seconds, sustained entirely by feedback
- **Character**: A scream that slowly evolves through harmonic feedback

### Whammy Bar Sections (~5:18):
- **Active**: Fuzz, Uni-Vibe, possibly Octavia
- **Technique**: Slow trill (hammer-on/pull-off) with simultaneous whammy bar pitch manipulation
- **Character**: Otherworldly, fluid, voice-like

### Feedback/Noise Passages:
- **Active**: Everything - maximum chaos
- **Technique**: Guitar facing amp stacks, controlling feedback with body position
- **Character**: Warfare soundscape, controlled noise

## 9. IMPLICATIONS FOR APP PRESET IMPLEMENTATION

### What We Already Have (in our app):
- Wah (CryBaby model, index 2) - CLOSE ENOUGH (Mayer mods are subtle frequency range tweaks)
- Univibe (index 20) - HAVE IT (our implementation models the 4-stage photocell circuit)
- Octavia (index 24) - HAVE IT (our ADAA-based octave-up fuzz)
- Fuzz Face (index 21) - HAVE IT but it models germanium PNP Fuzz Face, NOT the Axis Fuzz
- AmpModel Plexi voicing (index 3) - HAVE IT but does NOT model 6550/cold-bias characteristics

### What Needs Adjustment for Machine Gun Preset:

1. **Fuzz Face is WRONG circuit**: The BOG fuzz is NOT a germanium Fuzz Face. It's a silicon Axis Fuzz (Octavio-derived). Our Fuzz Face emulates the wrong topology entirely.
   - **Workaround**: Use the existing Fuzz Face with lower fuzz settings (silicon is tighter/less saggy than Ge) and higher volume
   - **Alternative**: The Overdrive (index 4) at high gain could approximate the tighter silicon character
   - **Ideal**: A new "Axis Fuzz" effect slot, but that's scope creep for now
   - **Best practical option**: Fuzz Face with adjusted params -- the key difference is Silicon=tighter/brighter/more attack vs Ge=saggy/warmer/spongier

2. **Amp Model**: Plexi voicing (index 3 in AmpModel) - WE HAVE THIS
   - Set to Plexi mode with mid-heavy EQ
   - Gain at moderate-high (most distortion comes from pedals, not amp)
   - **GAP**: Our Plexi model does NOT emulate the 6550 cold-bias Class B crossover distortion
   - The crossover distortion is the single biggest factor in the BOG "tight/percussive" character
   - To approximate: slightly higher gain + boosted mids + less bass than stock Plexi

3. **Cabinet**: Greenback IR (index 3 in CabinetSim) - WE HAVE THIS
   - **Note**: Hendrix likely had 75W Rolas or JBL D120F, NOT Greenbacks
   - These have flatter, more extended response than Greenbacks
   - For closer accuracy, the Jensen (index 8) or Alnico (index 7) might approximate the flatter JBL character better than Greenback

4. **Signal Chain Order**: Must reorder effects using setEffectOrder:
   - Wah(2) -> Octavia(24) -> FuzzFace(21) -> Univibe(20) -> AmpModel(5) -> CabinetSim(6)
   - This matches the confirmed BOG chain

5. **Tuning**: Full step down (D standard) - player concern, not an app setting

### Preset Parameter Estimates:
```
NoiseGate: enabled, threshold=-50dB
Wah: enabled, type=CryBaby, position=0.5 (user sweeps)
Octavia: enabled, fuzz=0.5, octave=0.7, volume=0.6
FuzzFace: enabled, fuzz=0.6, volume=0.75, guitarVolume=1.0 (lower fuzz to approximate Si tightness)
Univibe: enabled, speed=0.35, depth=0.7, mode=chorus, intensity=0.65 (chorus = safer default)
AmpModel: enabled, voicing=Plexi(3), gain=0.55, bass=0.35, mid=0.7, treble=0.45, presence=0.6
CabinetSim: enabled, cabinet=Greenback(3) or Jensen(8)
```

### Key Sonic Gaps We Cannot Emulate:
1. **6550 cold-bias crossover distortion** -- needs processTriode() modification for Class B notch
2. **Controlled speaker feedback** -- requires physical guitar-to-amp proximity, no DSP equivalent
3. **Real-time wah expression** -- user must sweep manually (no automation)
4. **Whammy bar pitch manipulation** -- physical guitar technique, not effects
5. **Behind-nut string bending** -- physical technique
6. **Axis Fuzz circuit** -- different topology from our Fuzz Face emulation
