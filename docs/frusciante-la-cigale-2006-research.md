# John Frusciante - La Cigale 2006 Intro Jam: Deep Tone Research

## Part 1: Performance Context

**Show**: Red Hot Chili Peppers at La Cigale, Paris, France
**Date**: April 20, 2006
**Tour**: Stadium Arcadium World Tour (pre-album release; SA dropped May 9, 2006)
**Setlist opener**: Intro Jam -> Can't Stop

La Cigale is a 1,400-capacity venue — extremely intimate for a band touring arenas.
The small room means Frusciante's amp volume was likely pulled back from arena
levels, which matters for the amp's breakup characteristics. At lower volumes, the
Marshall Major stays cleaner longer (200W into a small room), meaning more of the
tone character comes from the CE-1 preamp stage and the pedals rather than from
power tube saturation.

The intro jam is an improvised, atmospheric piece — spacey, delay-heavy, with
floating chords, arpeggiated figures, and Frusciante's signature mid-heavy warmth.
It showcases his "less is more" approach: clean-ish base tone with effects layered
on top for texture rather than gain.

## Part 2: Guitar

### Primary Instrument: 1962 Fender Stratocaster (Sunburst)

**Confidence: HIGH** — This was Frusciante's #1 guitar throughout the SA era.

- **Year**: 1962 (verified by multiple sources; some cite 1961-62)
- **Finish**: 3-tone sunburst, heavily worn (relic'd naturally)
- **Neck**: Rosewood fretboard (slab board, pre-1963 transition)
- **Pickups**: Original 1962 Fender single-coils
  - DC resistance: ~5.5-6.2K ohm (vintage spec, lower than modern)
  - AlNiCo V magnets (staggered polepieces)
  - Lower output than modern pickups — requires more amp gain for same volume
- **Bridge**: Synchronized tremolo (6-screw vintage), floating
- **Strings**: D'Addario .010-.046 gauge
- **Tuning**: Standard E (for this performance; some SA songs use alternate tunings)

### Pickup Position

**For the intro jam: NECK pickup (position 5) or NECK+MIDDLE (position 4)**

Reasoning:
- The La Cigale intro jam tone is warm, round, and mid-focused with rolled-off treble
- Frusciante overwhelmingly favors the neck pickup for clean/atmospheric passages
- The vintage '62 neck single-coil has a pronounced ~2-3kHz midrange peak with
  smooth treble rolloff above 4kHz — the classic "woman tone" frequency range
- His bridge pickup work is reserved for aggressive strumming (Can't Stop, etc.)
- Video evidence from this era shows neck pickup for atmospheric passages

### Guitar Volume & Tone Control Settings

**Volume**: 8-10 (mostly full up for atmospheric playing)
**Tone**: 6-8 (slightly rolled off for warmth, not fully bright)

Frusciante is known for using his guitar controls dynamically. Dave Lee (his tech)
confirmed that the amp was set to "just start to distort when you play hard" — so
volume knob position directly controls the clean/crunch transition.

## Part 3: Amplifiers

### Dual-Amp Configuration (Confirmed by Dave Lee, guitar tech)

Frusciante's 2006 rig used a **Boss CE-1 Chorus Ensemble as a signal splitter**
to feed two independent Marshall amplifier stacks simultaneously.

#### Amp 1: Marshall JMP Major 200W (Model 1967)
**Role**: Clean/warm foundation tone
**Signal source**: CE-1 direct output (unmodulated)

- **Model**: Marshall JMP Major, Model 1967, 200 watts
- **Tubes**: 4x KT88 power tubes (NOT the typical EL34 of other Marshalls)
- **Character**: Essentially a Marshall Plexi with double the headroom. KT88 tubes
  produce a tighter, more controlled low end than EL34s, with cleaner headroom
  and more hi-fi character. At moderate volumes, it stays remarkably clean.
- **Why it matters**: The 200W rating means this amp stays clean at volumes where a
  50W Plexi would be breaking up hard. This is Frusciante's "clean canvas."

**Approximate Settings** (from multiple sources, never officially published):
| Control    | Setting | Notes |
|------------|---------|-------|
| Presence   | 5-6     | Moderate presence, not harsh |
| Bass       | 8       | High bass — characteristic of his tone |
| Middle     | 5       | Moderate mids |
| Treble     | 6-7     | Slightly below center (lower than Jubilee) |
| Volume I   | 7-9     | Running hot but not into power amp breakup |

The high bass / moderate treble setting is critical — it produces the warm, thick
foundation that Frusciante's tone is known for. The vintage Strat's bright single-coils
are tamed by the amp's bass-heavy EQ, creating a balanced, warm output.

#### Amp 2: Marshall Silver Jubilee 2555 (25/50W or 25/55W)
**Role**: Crunchier, mid-heavy overdrive layer
**Signal source**: CE-1 chorus/effect output (modulated when chorus is engaged)

- **Model**: Marshall Silver Jubilee 2555, switchable 25/50W (or 25/55W variant)
- **Tubes**: EL34 power tubes
- **Key circuit feature**: Diode clipping in the preamp — this is what gives the Jubilee
  its characteristic smooth, compressed overdrive that differs from a pure tube Marshall
- **Character**: Smoother, more compressed gain than a Plexi. Rich midrange emphasis.
  The diode clipping adds sustain and compression without the fizzy top end of
  high-gain tube preamps.

**Approximate Settings**:
| Control    | Setting | Notes |
|------------|---------|-------|
| Lead/Rhythm | Lead channel | For overdriven passages |
| Gain       | 5-6     | Moderate — amp breaks up on hard picking |
| Bass       | 6-8     | High bass (see channel-linking note) |
| Middle     | 6       | Moderate-high mids |
| Treble     | 6       | Moderate |
| Presence   | 5       | Moderate |
| Master     | 5-7     | Pushed for power tube saturation |

**Channel Linking Technique**: Frusciante links channels 1 and 2 with a short patch
cable, then plugs into Channel 1's second input. This blends the two channels' voicings.
He sets the brighter channel volume to ~4 and the darker channel to ~7, creating a
blended tonality that is neither too bright nor too dark.

#### Third Amp (Confirmed by Dave Lee): "Slash Rig"
A second Silver Jubilee, run standalone with NO effects, set to an overdriven tone.
Used primarily with the Gibson Les Paul Custom for specific songs. **Not used for
the intro jam** — this is for heavy riff sections.

### Cabinets

- **6 Marshall 4x12 cabinets total**: 2 per amp head
- **Speakers**: Celestion G12T-75 (75W, 16 ohm per speaker)
  - Character: Warm midrange, controlled treble, smooth top end
  - Less "paperish" breakup than Greenbacks (G12M-25)
  - More headroom than Greenbacks — matches the high-wattage heads
  - Resonant frequency: ~75Hz
  - Sensitivity: ~97dB @ 1W/1m
- **Microphone setup** (studio/broadcast, relevant for the SBD recording):
  - Shure SM57 close-miked on cone edge
  - Royer R-121 ribbon mic at ~15 feet for room sound

### Mapping to Our App's Amp Models

The La Cigale intro jam tone is primarily the **Marshall Major (clean foundation)**
blended with the **Silver Jubilee (slight crunch layer)**. Since we cannot run two
amps simultaneously in our signal chain:

**Best single-amp approach**: Use our **Silver Jubilee voicing (modelType=5)** with
LOW inputGain (0.3-0.5). This captures the Jubilee's characteristic diode-clipping
midrange compression while staying in the clean-to-edge-of-breakup territory that
defines the intro jam tone.

The Major's contribution (clean headroom, tight bass) is approximated by keeping
the inputGain low — the Jubilee at low gain is cleaner and tighter than a Plexi
at low gain.

**Cabinet**: Use **G12T-75 (cabinetType not currently available — closest is
Greenback at index 4)**. The G12T-75 has more controlled highs than the Greenback,
so consider pairing with slightly lower treble/presence on the amp or EQ rolloff
above 4kHz.

### Signal Chain Order (Reconstructed)

The exact 2006 chain order is not officially published, but can be reliably
reconstructed from pedalboard photos, Dave Lee's comments, and standard
Frusciante routing:

```
Guitar
  -> Ibanez WH-10 V1 Wah (first in chain — wah before drive is JF standard)
  -> Boss DS-2 Turbo Distortion
  -> Mosrite FuzzRite
  -> EHX English Muff'n
  -> EHX Big Muff Pi
  -> MXR Micro Amp (clean boost, after drive section)
  -> Moog effects bank (MuRF, LPF, Phaser, Ring Mod — controlled via CP-251s)
  -> Guyatone VT-X Tremolo
  -> EHX Electric Mistress Flanger
  -> DOD 680 Analog Delay
  -> Line 6 DL-4 Delay Modeler
  -> EHX Holy Grail Reverb (spring mode)
  -> Boss CE-1 Chorus Ensemble (LAST before amps — acts as splitter)
     |-> Direct out -> Marshall Major (clean)
     |-> Effect out -> Marshall Silver Jubilee (crunch)
```

**Critical routing note**: The CE-1 is LAST in the chain before the amps. This is
confirmed by Dave Lee's interview with BOSS. It functions as:
1. A preamp (the CE-1's Level control sets the gain staging into the amps)
2. A signal splitter (two outputs to two amp heads)
3. Optionally, a chorus effect (when the chorus circuit is engaged)

Dave Lee specifically stated: "We would dial in the gain using [the CE-1 Level
control] to get to the point where it just starts to distort when you play hard.
That one knob was so much more important than the rest of his pedalboard."

## Part 5: The La Cigale Intro Jam — Specific Tone Analysis

### What We Hear

Listening to the La Cigale intro jam, the tone characteristics are:

1. **Clean-to-edge-of-breakup base**: Not distorted, but not sterile clean either.
   There's a warmth and slight compression that suggests the amp is on the verge
   of breaking up — exactly what the CE-1 preamp stage into the Marshall Major
   produces.

2. **Prominent delay**: Long, ambient delay with multiple repeats that create a
   washy, psychedelic atmosphere. The repeats are clearly audible and persist
   for several seconds, suggesting:
   - Delay time: ~400-500ms (dotted eighth or quarter note at moderate tempo)
   - Feedback: ~40-55% (enough for 5-7 audible repeats)
   - Mix: ~30-40% (delay is prominent but doesn't swamp the dry signal)

3. **Subtle chorus/modulation**: There's a gentle widening and movement to the
   tone that suggests the CE-1's chorus circuit is engaged, adding slow, subtle
   modulation. This is NOT a deep, obvious chorus — it's the CE-1's characteristic
   gentle warmth.

4. **Reverb wash**: Behind the delay, there's a reverb tail that adds depth.
   Likely the Holy Grail in spring mode, adding a natural spring-like ambience
   underneath the delay repeats.

5. **Mid-forward EQ**: The tone is decidedly mid-range focused — not scooped,
   not bright. This is the combination of:
   - Strat neck pickup (mid-emphasis)
   - Marshall Major's bass-heavy EQ with moderate treble
   - CE-1 preamp adding warmth
   - No treble-boosting or bright-cap effects

6. **Dynamic response**: Frusciante plays with significant dynamic variation —
   soft arpeggios that stay clean, harder chord stabs that push into slight
   breakup. This is the hallmark of the CE-1 preamp gain staging that Dave Lee
   described.

### Effects Active During Intro Jam

**CONFIRMED ACTIVE:**
- Boss CE-1 Chorus Ensemble: ON (always on — it's the signal splitter, and the
  gentle chorus adds the subtle modulation heard in the intro)
- Delay (likely Line 6 DL-4 or DOD 680): ON with long ambient settings
- EHX Holy Grail Reverb: Likely ON in spring mode for underlying ambience

**LIKELY INACTIVE:**
- Boss DS-2: OFF (no distortion heard in intro jam)
- EHX Big Muff: OFF
- FuzzRite: OFF
- English Muff'n: OFF
- WH-10 Wah: OFF (no wah sweep heard)
- MXR Micro Amp: Possibly ON for slight level boost, but unconfirmed
- Moog effects: Possibly — the MuRF or LPF could contribute to the atmospheric
  quality, but the tone doesn't exhibit obvious filter sweeps

### Delay Analysis — Which Pedal?

For the intro jam's atmospheric delay:

**Most likely: Line 6 DL-4** set to an analog delay model
- The DL-4 offers tape and analog delay emulations that darken repeats
- The long, washy character matches a modulated analog delay algorithm
- The DL-4's analog delay model has built-in modulation on the repeats that
  creates exactly the "swimming" quality heard in the intro jam
- Frusciante is documented using the DL-4 extensively from By the Way onward

**Alternative: DOD 680 Analog Delay**
- True analog circuit — repeats naturally darken and degrade
- Frusciante quote about the DOD: "I turned off the original signal and had an
  echo maybe half a second long, or a little longer. And set it to one repeat or
  a couple of repeats." — This describes a DIFFERENT usage (studio technique with
  dry signal muted), not the live intro jam approach
- The DOD 680's maximum delay time (~300ms) may be too short for the atmospheric
  quality heard in the intro jam

**Verdict**: The DL-4 is the primary delay for the intro jam, likely set to the
"Analog Delay" or "Tape Echo" model with ~400-500ms delay time, moderate-high
feedback (~45%), and modulation engaged.

### CE-1 Chorus Settings (Inference)

The CE-1 has limited controls:
- **Level**: Controls preamp gain going into the amps
  - Set to the "edge of breakup" point per Dave Lee
  - This varies by venue/volume but is approximately 60-70% of the knob range
- **Intensity** (chorus depth/rate): The CE-1 has a combined intensity control
  - For the atmospheric intro jam: MODERATE setting (~40-50%)
  - This produces a gentle, slow chorus that adds width without obvious warble
- **Chorus/Vibrato switch**: Set to CHORUS (not vibrato)
  - Chorus mode blends wet+dry; vibrato mode is 100% wet pitch modulation
  - The intro jam sound is clearly chorus (dry signal present) not vibrato

### Recommended Preset Configuration

```
Chain: [Compressor] -> [AmpModel] -> [CabinetSim] -> [Chorus] -> [Delay] -> [Reverb]
```

#### Compressor (index 1): ENABLED
Gentle OTA-style compression to approximate the CE-1 preamp's dynamic compression.
- threshold: -18 dB (gentle, catches peaks)
- ratio: 2.5:1 (light compression)
- attack: 15 ms (lets transients through)
- release: 200 ms (smooth release)
- makeupGain: 1.5 dB
- kneeWidth: 8 dB (soft knee)
- character: 1 (OTA mode — smoother, more vintage)

#### AmpModel (index 5): ENABLED
Silver Jubilee voicing at low gain to approximate the Major/Jubilee blend.
- inputGain: 0.35 (very low — edge of breakup territory)
- outputLevel: 0.65
- modelType: 5 (Silver Jubilee)
- variac: 0 (off)

Why 0.35 inputGain: The Jubilee's internal preGain is 6.0x. At inputGain=0.35,
effective drive = 0.35 * 6.0 = 2.1x through the cascaded tanh stages. This
keeps the signal in the "just starts to distort on hard picking" zone that
Dave Lee described, while staying clean on soft passages.

#### CabinetSim (index 6): ENABLED
- cabinetType: 4 (Greenback — closest to G12T-75's warm midrange)
- mix: 1.0

#### Chorus (index 8): ENABLED
CE-1 mode with subtle settings to replicate the gentle widening.
- rate: 0.5 Hz (slow, gentle movement)
- depth: 0.45 (moderate — the CE-1 is not a deep chorus)
- mode: 1 (CE-1 Chorus Ensemble)
- wetDryMix: 0.35 (blended, not overpowering)

#### Delay (index 12): ENABLED
Long atmospheric delay — the signature of this intro jam.
- delayTimeMs: 450 ms (between quarter and dotted eighth at ~133 BPM)
- feedback: 0.45 (multiple repeats, decaying naturally over ~5 seconds)
- wetDryMix: 0.35 (delay is prominent but doesn't obscure playing)

**Limitation**: Our delay is a simple digital delay without modulated repeats.
The DL-4's analog delay model adds subtle pitch modulation to repeats that
darkens and "smears" them over time. To partially compensate, we could:
1. Accept the cleaner digital repeat character (still sounds good)
2. Future enhancement: add a "tape/analog" mode with LPF on feedback path
   and subtle chorus modulation on the delay line

#### Reverb (index 13): ENABLED
Simulating the Holy Grail's spring mode contribution.
- decay: 2.5 s (medium-long tail)
- damping: 0.4 (moderate — spring reverbs have some HF absorption)
- preDelay: 15 ms (short pre-delay, spring character)
- size: 0.5 (medium room)
- wetDryMix: 0.30 (sits underneath the delay, adds depth without dominating)

## Part 7: What This Preset CANNOT Replicate

### Fundamental Limitations

1. **Dual-amp blending**: The real tone is two amps (Major clean + Jubilee crunch)
   playing simultaneously, each with different EQ and gain characteristics. The
   listener hears the sum of both. Our single amp model cannot replicate this
   stereo-to-mono blended character. The Major provides clean body; the Jubilee
   adds mid-range crunch. Together they create a tone that is both clean AND
   slightly dirty at the same time — a quality that a single amp path cannot match.

2. **CE-1 preamp character**: The Boss CE-1's discrete JFET/BJT preamp stage adds
   harmonic content and soft compression that is distinct from any generic
   compressor. Dave Lee explicitly stated this was the single most important
   tone-shaping element after Frusciante's hands and pickups.

3. **Analog delay darkening**: The DL-4's analog/tape delay models add LPF and
   modulation to the feedback path, so each repeat is progressively darker and
   more "smeared" than the last. Our digital delay has pristine repeats. This is
   the single biggest tonal difference from the original.

4. **Vintage Strat pickups**: Real 1962 Fender pickups have specific scatter-wound
   coil characteristics, aged magnets (partially demagnetized AlNiCo V), and
   microphonic qualities that modern pickups don't replicate. This affects the
   fundamental harmonic content before any pedals or amps.

5. **Room interaction at La Cigale**: The small, acoustically reflective venue
   contributes to the recording's ambience. The SBD recording captures some of
   this via room mics.

6. **Dynamic playing technique**: Frusciante's right-hand dynamics are the #1
   tone factor according to Dave Lee. No amount of gear settings can replicate
   the feel of his playing — the way he controls volume and breakup through
   pick attack and guitar volume knob manipulation.

### Enhancement Roadmap

To get closer to the La Cigale tone in future updates:

1. **Analog Delay Mode** (highest impact): Add a feedback-path LPF + subtle
   modulation option to the Delay effect. Parameters: LP cutoff (800-4000Hz),
   modulation rate (0.3-2Hz), modulation depth (0-5ms). This would darken repeats
   progressively like a real BBD or tape echo.

2. **Dual-Amp Mode**: Allow two AmpModel instances in parallel with independent
   settings and a blend control. Major (clean, bass-heavy) + Jubilee (crunch,
   mid-heavy) would nail the Frusciante rig.

3. **G12T-75 Cabinet IR**: The Greenback is close but not identical. The G12T-75
   has more controlled treble (gentler rolloff above 4kHz) and slightly less
   midrange peak than the Greenback.
